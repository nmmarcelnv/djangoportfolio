We all want to be healthy, don't we? Unfortunately, millions of individuals around the world live with some sort of disease condition, be it heart disease, HIV/AIDS, Cancer, Cholera, you name it. For the purpose of raising awareness, the World Health Organization (WHO) collects data on disease burden and cause of deaths in different countries around the world and the data is made available in their data repository as Excel files.

It's one thing to collect data but what good is it if we can't make good use it. First, I wanted to be able to quickly view top killer diseases in any given country using survey data collected by the WHO. Since these are combersome Excel files with hundreds of columns and rows, I decided to write a code that downloads the data from the WHO, cleans it, retrieves the top ten killer diseases for the country requested, and creates a plot for visualization.

In the future, I would like extend this project to perform more interesting tasks such as comparing mortality rates between regions, and intergrating different health related datasets to find correlations between mortality and socio-economic factors, mortality and environmental factors, and mortality and life styles. If you're a public health student or professional, I hope you find something useful here. If you have cool ideas to improve people's health, I would love to hear from you. For now, you're just one click away from discovering the top killer diseases in your region. Have fun and be healthy!
